Onigiri Recipe

Description

This a recipe to make a japanese dish known as Onigiri, american known as rice balls. They're very simple to make an super duper delicious, here is a simple tutorial on how to make these lovely little things for lunch, dinner, or because you just want some rice balls!

Ingredients

Here are some things you'll need before we start cooking

  • 2/3 cups of Short-grain rice, rinsed off
  • 2/3 cup water
  • 'Furikake' a japanese seasoning used for rice balls

    • -This isn't completely neccessary but it is included in the recipe.

  • 3 sheets of 'nori' also known as dry seeweed (1-inch strips preffered)
  • Salt (to your preference)
  • 'shiso with hijiki' also known as dried seaweed flakes (as many as you prefer)

Instructions

Finally lets get cooking, see the steps below to learn how to make these lovely snacks.

  1. Cook your rice to a soft texture like sushi rice, a rice cooker is reccommended for the best results.
  2. Pop your cooked rice into a nice mixing bowl. Add the furikake or rice flavouring and mix through evenly. Separate the rice into equal portions, big enough to be a large handful each.
  3. Wet your hands with water and rub together with a pinch or two of salt. This stops the rice sticking to your hands and helps keep it fresher for longer.
  4. Take up one portion of rice in your hands.
  5. If you are hiding some fillings inside, here is where you make an indent, place the ingredients inside and fold the rice over, then lightly press into a ball.
